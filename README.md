## 2. WHAT IS SAKI SANOBASHI?

Let's start from the beginning.

**Saki Sanobashi**, also known as **Go For A Punch (GFAP)**, is the name given to a supposedly lost Japanese animated short (OVA) that may or may not have existed sometime in the late 1980s.

The story, as described by people who claim to have seen it:

A group of schoolgirls â€” the number varies between witnesses, but nine is frequently mentioned â€” are trapped inside a large room. The room is most often described as a bathroom or bathhouse, with white tiles, sinks, and no visible exits. There are no doors. There are no windows.

The girls don't know how they got there. As time passes, they slowly lose hope. They begin to starve. They hold philosophical conversations about the nature of existence, life, death, and whether they will ever be rescued.

Then everything falls apart.

The girls begin to scream. They remove their clothing. And then they start dying â€” some bashing their heads against walls or sinks, some scratching their own necks until they bleed out, some drowning each other. One girl is held against a sink by another at her own request, because she can't bring herself to do it alone.

The video reportedly has English subtitles. The visual quality is degraded â€” it looks like an old VHS recording. The animation style is amateurish, similar to low-budget 1980s OVA productions. There are no credits. The audio is Japanese. The title, as shown on the video, was something along the lines of "Go for a punch" followed by garbled symbols (probably mojibake â€” character encoding corruption that happens on computers not configured for Japanese text).

**That is the legend.**
